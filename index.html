Griffenflies

Meganeura, one of the largest Griffenflies common throught the Carboniferous and Permian.

Traits

These large areal preditores have advanced compound eyes designed to scan for potential victims.
They also have long grasping arms for grabing small animals and exoskelital teeth for disecting them quickly.
This all rolled into a body that can have a wingspan of 28 inches accross or over 2 feet.

Existence

First evolving around 300 million years ago,
the group spead across the northern hemisphere with fossils found in both Europe and North America.
These creatures would remain domiant for 70 milion years
before eventualy being out competed completly by the pterasaurs in the late Triassic.

Distinction

While highly resembling dragonflies, griffenflies are not all that related to other similar looking insects.
The main difference between them and dragonflies besides size is a changed wing venation system.
